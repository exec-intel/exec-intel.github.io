Execution Intelligence

Synopsis

Functional Summary

Execution Intelligence (EI) is a layer that sits between an organization's Annual Operating Plan and the people responsible for delivering it. It ingests the plan — goals, KPIs, and how they cascade across teams — and tracks commitments captured in natural language during normal reviews, rather than through forms or spreadsheets.

It follows up automatically against agreed review cadences, flags deviations against actual data, and routes exceptions for a decision instead of letting them pass unnoticed. Every commitment, override, and outcome is recorded, which lets the system surface the closest relevant precedent when a similar situation recurs — so judgment about what's likely to work compounds over time rather than resetting each cycle.

EI does not make decisions on its own. It makes execution continuously visible and prompts the people accountable for it to act.

Technical Summary

The system combines a deterministic core with AI at two specific points. The core is a knowledge graph — the plan, its KPIs, and their dependencies — and an immutable ledger that logs every commitment and outcome as it happens. Reconciliation, tolerance checks, approval routing, and scheduling all run as rule-based logic against this data, not as model output.

AI is used for ingestion — parsing a plan document into structured graph entries, confirmed by a person before anything goes live — and for capture — turning a commitment typed in plain language into a structured, tagged record.

Historical judgment compounds through retrieval, not fine-tuning: overrides of past patterns are logged as scored hypotheses, and the system retrieves the closest relevant precedent from an organization's own data, rather than relying on a model trained on it. The platform is model-agnostic, integrating with whichever LLM an organization already has under contract.
